Abstract

This study looks at the incarceration rates between people of color and those who are white. This data looks at a variety of demographics of those that are incarcerated such as race, gender, and age. This report will primarily explore how people of different races are impacted by incarceration rates in the United States.

In this report, we will be identifying the injustices and the inequities that people of color often face when incarcerated. We will first look the overall picture of incarceration rates and see the steady growth of people going to jail between 1970 to 2018. In addition, we will be identifying how different races are impacted in different cities through the use of data visualizations. When analyzing some of the results, we notice that people of color are the direct stakeholders, since they are more often punished by this system of injustice. In hopes of fighting these inequities, we hope that this data helps to motivate people to push for changes in policy that disproportionately impact people of color.
